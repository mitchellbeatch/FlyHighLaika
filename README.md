On November 3rd, 1957, a stray dog found on the streets of Moscow was strapped into a tiny capsule called Sputnik 2 and launched into orbit. Her name was Laika. She was chosen partly because stray dogs were considered tough — already used to the cold, to hunger, to surviving on nothing. Soviet scientists figured she could handle it.
She was the first living creature to orbit Earth. She never came home.
The mission was never designed for her return. The technology to bring her back didn't exist yet, and there were no plans to develop it in time. She died a few hours into the flight, likely from overheating, though the Soviet government didn't admit this for decades. For a long time they said she lived for days, peaceful, before running out of oxygen.
Laika orbited Earth 2,570 times before Sputnik 2 burned up in the atmosphere in April 1958. She was somewhere between 3 and 5 years old.

**The Game**
This game asks a simple question: what if she made it?
You guide Laika's ship through the digital void — past the cascading radiation of distant stars rendered as falling code, climbing higher with each level as the storm grows faster and more relentless. She left Earth behind. The further she gets, the faster it falls.
The matrix rain isn't decoration. It's the universe doing what it does — indifferent, constant, beautiful, and deadly. Your job is just to keep her moving.

**How to Play**
Move your mouse to steer the ship
Avoid the falling characters — each hit drains her shields
Survive each 15-second level to keep climbing
The rain speeds up every level
When the shields hit zero, that's it
